Self-Care Guide

Is This Normal?

Ever stared at your period and wondered "Is this normal?" You aren't alone. Let's talk about your body with zero embarrassment.

The Period Palette

Menstrual blood doesn’t always look the same, and that’s okay.

Bright Red

Common during the first few days. Usually means a healthy, steady flow.

Dark Red / Brown

Older blood that took longer to leave the uterus—completely normal.

Pink

Can happen when blood mixes with cervical fluid on lighter days.

Gray / Unusual

This is not normal. Consult a doctor immediately as it could indicate an infection.

Blood Clots: Normal?

Small Clots (Coin-sized)

Common during heavy flow days and usually harmless.

Large or Frequent Clots

May be a sign of hormonal imbalance. A consultation is recommended.

When Should You See a Doctor?

Doctors hear these concerns every day. No embarrassment needed.

Severe or worsening pain
Extremely heavy bleeding
Periods stop for months
Strong odor or discharge

A Gentle Reminder

Every body is different. What’s normal for someone else may not be normal for you. Paying attention to your own pattern is the key. Luna is here to help you feel confident.
