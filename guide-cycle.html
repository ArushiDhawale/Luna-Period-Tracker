Menstrual Health

The Menstrual Cycle 101

The menstrual cycle is a natural monthly process in which the body prepares for pregnancy. It involves hormonal changes that regulate menstruation, ovulation, energy levels, mood, and overall well-being.

Typical duration: 21–35 days

Cycle Phases

Menstrual Phase

Days 1–5

Physiology

  • The uterus sheds its lining, causing bleeding.
  • Estrogen and progesterone levels are at their lowest.
Common: Cramps, fatigue, low energy, mood sensitivity.

Self-Care Protocol

  • Prioritize rest and hydration.
  • Consume iron-rich foods (spinach, dates).
  • Gentle stretching or light yoga.

Follicular Phase

Days 1–13

Physiology

  • Hormone levels begin to rise.
  • The body prepares an egg for release.
Common: Improved energy, clear thinking, increased motivation.

Self-Care Protocol

  • Excellent time to start new projects.
  • Focus on balanced, protein-rich meals.
  • Engage in light to moderate exercise.

Ovulation Phase

Around Day 14

Physiology

  • An egg is released from the ovary.
  • Fertility is at its peak.
Common: High energy, confidence, social mood.

Self-Care Protocol

  • Stay highly hydrated.
  • Handle higher intensity workouts.
  • Track ovulation if planning.

Luteal Phase

Days 15–28

Physiology

  • Significant hormonal shifts occur.
  • The body prepares for the next cycle.
Common: PMS, mood swings, bloating, lower energy.

Self-Care Protocol

  • Reduce caffeine and sugar intake.
  • Eat magnesium-rich foods.
  • Focus on mindfulness and rest.
