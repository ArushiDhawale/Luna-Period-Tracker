Menstrual Health

Hygiene Products

There is no single “best” menstrual hygiene product — only what feels safe, comfortable, and right for your body and lifestyle. Pads, tampons, and menstrual cups each have their own benefits. Understanding how they work can help you make informed choices.

Sanitary Pads

Pads are worn outside the body and absorb menstrual blood as it flows. They come in different sizes and absorbency levels.

Benefits

  • Easy to use; requires no insertion.
  • Excellent option for beginners.
  • No risk of Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS).

Limitations

  • Can feel bulky or shift during movement.
  • May cause irritation if not changed often.

Safe Use Tips

Change pads every 4–6 hours, keep the area dry, and choose breathable materials to reduce discomfort.

Tampons

Tampons are inserted into the vagina and absorb blood before it leaves the body.

Benefits

  • Discreet and comfortable.
  • Allows for swimming and physical activity.
  • Greater freedom of movement.

Limitations

  • Small risk of TSS if left in too long.
  • May cause dryness for some users.

Safe Use Tips

  • Use the lowest absorbency needed.
  • Change every 4–8 hours.
  • Never sleep with a tampon for more than 8 hours.

Menstrual Cups

Menstrual cups are reusable silicone or rubber cups inserted into the vagina to collect blood instead of absorbing it.

Benefits

  • Eco-friendly and cost-effective.
  • Can be worn for 8–12 hours.
  • Collects rather than absorbs.

Limitations

  • Requires proper cleaning.
  • Steep learning curve for insertion.

Safe Use Tips

Sterilize the cup between cycles, wash hands before insertion/removal, and clean thoroughly after each use.

Understanding Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS)

TSS is a rare but serious condition caused by bacterial toxins. It has most commonly been linked to improper tampon use, though it is extremely rare.

How to Reduce Risk:

  • Change tampons regularly.
  • Avoid overnight use beyond recommended hours.
  • Practice good hand hygiene.
  • Switch to pads or cups occasionally.

If you experience sudden fever, rash, dizziness, or vomiting during menstruation, seek medical help immediately.

Which Product Is Best?

The best menstrual product is the one that fits your body, flow, and lifestyle. Many people switch products during their cycle — using pads at night, tampons during the day, or cups for longer wear. There’s no right or wrong choice.

A Gentle Reminder

Menstrual hygiene is personal. What works for someone else may not work for you — and that’s completely okay. Comfort, safety, and confidence matter most.
